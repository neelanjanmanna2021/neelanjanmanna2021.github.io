    Frequently Asked Questions

    Is RPL legal in Australia?

    Yes, Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) is legal and widely accepted in Australia. The Australian government recognizes RPL as a legitimate pathway for individuals to obtain qualifications and certifications. RPL is governed by the Australian Skills Quality Authority (ASQA), which ensures that RPL processes and assessments meet national standards. Many educational institutions, training providers, and industry bodies in Australia offer RPL as a means for individuals to gain recognition and credit for their existing knowledge, skills, and experience.

    What is a RPL?

    RPL stands for Recognition of Prior Learning. As the name suggests, RPL providers such as Qualify Me can provide you with a nationally recognised qualification simply by assessing your prior work experience. There is no classroom study and zero interruptions to your work and life schedule. Recognition of Prior Learning assesses what you already know and checking if it matches the qualification you want to obtain. The best part is that it takes into consideration formal, non-formal and informal learning.

    Are RPL Qualifications Nationally Recognised In Australia?

    Yes, RPL qualifications are nationally recognized in Australia. RPL provides individuals with the opportunity to receive formal recognition for their existing knowledge and skills, regardless of how they were acquired. These qualifications are acknowledged and accepted across the country, ensuring that individuals can confidently present their RPL credentials to prospective employers, educational institutions, or industry regulatory bodies.

    How does RPL work?

    RPL works by evaluating your existing knowledge, skills, and experience to see if they match the requirements of a particular qualification. You gather evidence, such as work samples or certifications, to show what you already know and can do. Trained assessors review the evidence and decide if it meets the necessary standards for recognition. If it does, you can get credit for what you’ve already learned, which can help you complete your qualification faster or skip certain parts of the program. RPL offers a flexible and personalized way of learning that recognizes and validates the skills and knowledge you already have.

    How much does the RPL cost?

    The cost of Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) varies depending on several factors such as the type of qualification, the assessment process, and the provider offering the RPL service. It is best to contact with us to inquire about the cost.

    How long does it take to get RPL assessed?

    The duration to get RPL can vary depending on the industry you are focusing on, the certification, the evidence you supply, and how efficiently you supply the relevant evidence. Overall, the RPL process can take from 1 to a maximum of 8 weeks.

    How to get rpl certificate?

    To receive your RPL certificate, all you need to do is apply to Qualify Me. You’ll need to provide evidence to support your application. This may include documents such as a report or a portfolio of work samples, or evidence of assessment activities, such as work observations or interviews.

    What are the benefits of RPL?

    The benefits of RPL include time and cost savings, accelerated qualification attainment, enhanced career opportunities, increased confidence, customized learning experience, and promotion of lifelong learning.
